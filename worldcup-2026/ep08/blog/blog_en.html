🇧🇪 Belgium — The Last Blaze of the Golden Generation

Everyone knows 2026 is the last ride. Kevin De Bruyne (35 years old) is still one of the best midfielders on the planet. The vision and creativity he has shown at Manchester City remains unmatched. Romelu Lukaku stepped away from national duty, but Leandro Trossard and Johan Bakayoko carry the attack. A young Man City generation anchors the defense, completing a successful generational handover.

The Bronze Medal pain of 2018, the humiliation of the 2022 group exit — they haven't forgotten. 2026 is genuinely the last chance. Belgium is desperate, and that desperation may be their greatest weapon.

⭐ Ace: Kevin De Bruyne (Manchester City · 35 · still one of the world's best MFs)   🔥 Attack: Leandro Trossard (Arsenal) · Johan Bakayoko (Atlético Madrid)
🎯 Style: Defensive shape + fast counter-attack · set-piece threat   ⚠️ Weakness: No true No. 9 after Lukaku's departure

🇮🇹 Italy — The Modern Evolution of Catenaccio

2006 champions, 2018 qualifying disaster, 2022 rebuild. Manager Luciano Spalletti has completed the generational transition. The engine: Nicolo Barella — Inter Milan's heartbeat and midfield commander. Sandro Tonali has fully recovered from his controversies and is back in dominant form. Federico Chiesa and Giovanni Di Lorenzo drive the attack wide and deep.

Italy's strength is defensive organization and tactical adaptability — the modern evolution of catenaccio. Their ability to keep clean sheets against top opposition remains elite. If they can find their goal-scoring consistency, they could go very deep in this tournament.

⭐ Ace: Nicolo Barella (Inter Milan · midfield commander)   🔥 MF: Sandro Tonali (Newcastle · fully revived) · Manuel Locatelli
🎯 Attack: Federico Chiesa (Liverpool) · Giovanni Di Lorenzo (Napoli)   ⚠️ Weakness: CF finishing — no clear-cut striker at the 9

🇲🇦 Morocco & 🇰🇷 South Korea — The Upset Artists

Remember the miracle of Morocco at Qatar 2022? They beat Spain, Portugal, and Belgium back-to-back to become Africa's first-ever semi-finalists in World Cup history. Hakim Ziyech still leads the charge in 2026. The massive North African diaspora in Los Angeles creates a genuine home atmosphere for Morocco. If they maintain the same intensity and defensive solidarity as 2022, another round of upsets is entirely plausible.

South Korea arrives with their golden generation: Lee Kang-in (PSG's creative playmaker) and Son Heung-min (Tottenham legend, potentially in his final World Cup) form the most dangerous attacking pair in Korean football history. If you remember their stunning comeback against Australia in 2022, you know — never count Korea out. A quarter-final run is entirely possible if both stars fire together.

⚡ Group E: 3 Key Variables

VARIABLE 01 — Belgium vs Italy in the Group Opener
Historical head-to-head: Italy 12 wins, Belgium 8 wins, 6 draws. The loser faces serious jeopardy for their round-of-16 chances. This is the Group E match of the tournament — the result will dictate the entire group's dynamic.
VARIABLE 02 — Morocco's 2022 Repeat Potential
The massive North African diaspora in Los Angeles gives Morocco a genuine home-crowd advantage. Maintaining 2022's defensive intensity against Belgium or Italy is entirely achievable — and could flip the whole group.
VARIABLE 03 — Son & Lee Kang-in Firing Together
If Son Heung-min and Lee Kang-in are both in top form simultaneously, a Korean quarter-final run becomes real. The 2022 Australia comeback proved it — Korea is never a team to dismiss lightly.
