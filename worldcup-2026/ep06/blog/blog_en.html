🟢 Brazil — The Seleção Hunting Star #6

After the shock of their 2022 Qatar quarterfinal exit, Brazil has rebuilt. Vinícius Júnior claimed the Ballon d'Or at Real Madrid and enters this tournament at 27 — his absolute peak. This is his World Cup.

⭐ Ace: Vinícius Júnior (Real Madrid · Age 27 · Ballon d'Or)   🔥 Attack: Rodrygo · Gabriel Martinelli (Arsenal)
🛡️ Defensive Captain: Marquinhos (PSG)   🎯 Tactics: 4-2-3-1 — defensive solidity, then explosive counters

🔵 England — 60 Years of Hurt Ends Here?

The birthplace of football last lifted the World Cup in 1966. Jude Bellingham (Real Madrid, 23) has grown into the next generation's best midfielder. Harry Kane (31) — England's all-time top scorer — is in the final golden chapter of his career. Phil Foden and Bukayo Saka round out a squad that looks complete on paper.

💙 Heartbeat: Jude Bellingham (Real Madrid · Age 23)   ⚽ Striker: Harry Kane (Bayern Munich · Age 31)
🌟 Wingers: Phil Foden (Man City) · Bukayo Saka (Arsenal)   ⚠️ Challenge: Overcoming the big-game mental block from 2022 & 2020

🇯🇵 Japan & 🇪🇨 Ecuador

Japan proved they're not "just Asian" at Qatar 2022, eliminating both Spain and Germany from the group stage. Kaoru Mitoma (Brighton) leads a more experienced and Europe-hardened squad in 2026. Their 5-4-1 defensive block into high-speed counter is a proven giant-killer system — England isn't immune.

Ecuador may be ranked 42nd, but they've been a consistent CONMEBOL qualifier for two decades. With Enner Valencia up front, they're capable of troubling Brazil's defense in their opening match — one result that could reshape the entire group.

⚡ Three Variables That Decide Group C

① Brazil vs England — June 17, Dallas
Head-to-head: Brazil 8 wins, England 3 wins. The loser faces a psychological collapse. This is the de facto group final.
② Japan's Upset Potential — The 2022 System
If their Qatar-style defensive counter-attack clicks against England, a Group 2nd place is very much achievable. They have the tactical DNA to shock.
③ Ecuador's Opening Shock vs Brazil
One Valencia strike could upend the entire group standings. Don't sleep on them in game one.
