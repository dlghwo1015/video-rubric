🇺🇸 USA Analysis — Host Nation's Dream

USA is the true protagonist of 2026. Christian Pulisic leading the charge, Folarin Balogun's clinical finishing, Weston McKennie's relentless energy. Beyond the host advantage, a generation hardened by Europe's biggest clubs. USMNT is no longer a dark horse — they've arrived with the face of genuine contenders.

2022 Qatar: Round of 16 (fell to Netherlands). This time is different — home advantage, a more mature squad, and four years of growth. The goal is crystal clear: USA's first-ever World Cup quarterfinal.

🇸🇳 Senegal Analysis — After Mané

Senegal returns from the pain of 2022, hungrier than ever. Even as Sadio Mané's era fades, Ismaila Sarr and Pape Sarr usher in a new generation. Africa's most physical team, lightning-fast counters, iron-clad defensive organization.

Any team that underestimates Senegal will pay the price. The same squad that troubled England in 2022 has returned — more experienced, more dangerous.

🇨🇴 Colombia & 🇷🇸 Serbia Analysis

Colombia: They have the magic of Luís Díaz — Liverpool's ace, dribbling and finishing fused into one. James Rodríguez's twilight, Díaz's dawn. Colombia is the 2026 World Cup's greatest dark horse with a generational handover complete.

Serbia: Dušan Vlahović and Aleksandar Mitrović provide explosive goalscoring threat. But defensive concentration lapses and the striker selection feud remain the key variables.
