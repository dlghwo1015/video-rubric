🇹🇷 Turkey — The Arda Güler Era Begins

In 2026, Turkey arrives as a completely transformed team. Arda Güler — Real Madrid's 21-year-old genius. He debuted in the Champions League in 2024 and his dribbling, shooting, and vision passing are already world-class. Flanking him is Kerem Aktürkoğlu from Galatasaray, one of Europe's most dangerous wingers.

⭐ Arda Güler — Real Madrid, 21, world's best young talent  |  ⚡ Kerem Aktürkoğlu — Galatasaray, wing destroyer  |  🛡️ UEFA-seasoned defence

Turkey's best-ever World Cup finish is 3rd place (2002 Korea/Japan). In 2026, Güler's generation is declaring that record will fall. Seeded first in Group I, this is the strongest Turkish squad in history — and they know it.

🇳🇬 Nigeria — Osimhen, Africa's Golden Bullet

Nigeria's game plan starts and ends with Victor Osimhen. Napoli's golden bullet — 30-plus goals in each of two consecutive Serie A seasons — he is one of the best strikers on the planet right now. His injury history is the one red flag; a healthy Osimhen can carry Nigeria past the group stage and threaten the quarterfinals.

🔥 Victor Osimhen — Napoli, back-to-back 30+ Serie A goals, Africa's No. 1 striker  |  💪 Frank Onoh — midfield anchor  |  ⚡ Simon Aiyolan — flank threat

The Super Eagles boast a solid midfield behind their star striker. The entire team's fate depends on one man's fitness — that is Nigeria's only real weakness, and the biggest variable in Group I.

🇦🇺 Australia & 🇮🇷 Iran — Chasing the Miracle

Australia carries the Qatar 2022 miracle in their DNA. Mathew Leckie's warrior spirit and a disciplined 4-3-3 that never relents — the Socceroos' greatest weapon is refusing to die. Keep the defensive shape intact for three games and third place is within reach.

Iran enter with Sardar Azmoun and Asia's most suffocating defensive system. They held England, the USA, and Wales in 2022 — never write off the Iranians. Azmoun's fitness is the single factor that determines how far Iran can go.
